# Public Domain Design & Legal Compliance

This document explains how the project integrates public-domain and classical/philosophical sources into the game's narrative and design while maintaining legal compliance and clarity for contributors.

Purpose
- Document the provenance and licensing decisions for narrative elements derived from classical philosophy, myth, and 19th-century literature.
- Provide guidance to contributors so that art, prose, and mechanics remain free of third-party copyrighted material and safe to redistribute under the project's CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license.

Included sources & intent
- Classical philosophy: selected short quotations and paraphrases from Seneca and Epicurus are used for thematic flavor. Only content that is unambiguously in the public domain (classical texts in original-language translations that are public domain) or rewritten in original prose is used.
- Mythological archetypes: motifs like "Thanatos' inverted torch" are used as archetypal imagery and are not copied from any modern copyrighted depiction.
- 19th-century literature: elements inspired by Poe and Christina Rossetti are carefully reinterpreted and rephrased to avoid direct quotations from copyrighted modern translations; contributors should only use public-domain translations or write original text.

Contributor guidance
- Do not paste modern copyrighted translations or text into the repository. When in doubt, link to the source and summarize in original words.
- For flavor text, prefer short original prose written by contributors or public-domain translations (cite source and edition).
- Artwork and icons must be original or clearly licensed for reuse (public domain, CC0, or compatible permissive licenses). Avoid assets bundled from proprietary sources.

Attribution & licensing
- The repository and its contents are distributed under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0. Contributions that incorporate third-party public-domain content must still include attribution where appropriate and a citation in the relevant doc or asset metadata.
- If a contributor adds a public-domain translation or image, include a citation in the commit message and the asset metadata (filename or adjacent .meta file).

Review process
- All narrative and art contributions that reference classical or literary sources must be flagged in the PR description with the provenance and a confirmation that the material is public domain or original.
- The project maintainers will review for compliance before merging.
